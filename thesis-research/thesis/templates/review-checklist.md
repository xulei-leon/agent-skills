# Review Checklist — Quality Criteria for Academic Papers

> Use this checklist when reviewing a paper draft. Rate each criterion and provide specific evidence.

---

## A. Clarity & Readability

- [ ] **A1.** Can a non-expert in the sub-field follow the main argument?
- [ ] **A2.** Is the abstract self-contained and accurate?
- [ ] **A3.** Are all acronyms defined on first use?
- [ ] **A4.** Is terminology consistent throughout all sections?
- [ ] **A5.** Does each paragraph have a clear topic sentence?
- [ ] **A6.** Are sentences concise (no run-ons over 40 words)?

## B. Structure & Flow

- [ ] **B1.** Does the introduction follow the funnel structure (broad → narrow → specific)?
- [ ] **B2.** Does the conclusion mirror the introduction (summary → contributions → future)?
- [ ] **B3.** Are sections ordered logically (no jarring jumps between topics)?
- [ ] **B4.** Are transitions between sections smooth?
- [ ] **B5.** Does the related work section group papers thematically (not listed one-by-one)?
- [ ] **B6.** Does the paper roadmap in the introduction match the actual paper structure?

## C. Content Completeness

- [ ] **C1.** Introduction: Is the problem statement specific and well-motivated?
- [ ] **C2.** Introduction: Are contributions stated clearly and verifiably?
- [ ] **C3.** Related Work: Is the gap statement explicit and well-supported?
- [ ] **C4.** Related Work: Are all major competing approaches covered?
- [ ] **C5.** Methodology: Is the approach reproducible from the description?
- [ ] **C6.** Methodology: Are assumptions stated explicitly?
- [ ] **C7.** Experiments: Are datasets and metrics standard for the field?
- [ ] **C8.** Experiments: Are baselines appropriate and fairly compared?
- [ ] **C9.** Experiments: Do results answer the research questions?
- [ ] **C10.** Experiments: Is statistical significance addressed?
- [ ] **C11.** Conclusion: Are limitations honest and specific?
- [ ] **C12.** Conclusion: Does future work suggest meaningful steps?
